About Retrofit for the Future

Retrofit for the Future catalysed the retrofit of over 100 homes across the UK, with an ambition of achieving an 80% reduction in the in-use CO2 emissions of each property. Coordinated by the Technology Strategy Board , the Retrofit for the Future competition encouraged collaboration between housing providers, designers, contractors and researchers, and has helped to stimulate new business opportunities in the retrofit market.

Retrofit for the Future data and analysis

This website hosts the first analysis of monitoring data provided by Retrofit for the Future projects. Here you will find a series of charts showing the performance of the properties, including energy use, CO2 emissions, internal temperatures, air tightness and occupant satisfaction.

Data on individual Retrofit for the Future projects can be accessed through the embed database . The cleaned raw sensor data for the properties are available here.

From this site, you can also download Retrofit Revealed The Retrofit for the Future projects – data analysis report , which summarises the data analysis and provides a qualitative assessment of the final reports produced by Retrofit for the Future project teams. It contains valuable and practical findings for anyone planning a retrofit project.

Retrofit for the Future: results and findings

Data provided by Retrofit for the Future projects shows that:

  • Energy consumption and CO2 emissions can be massively reduced by retrofitting homes
  • Retrofit can improve occupants’ comfort and satisfaction with their homes
  • Residents in retrofitted properties are better able to achieve comfortable living conditions at a reasonable cost

You can use the charts on this webpage to find out about the improvements achieved by the Retrofit for the Future projects.

Retrofit for the Future also encouraged innovation and experimentation, and we saw many examples of industry and clients working together to effect change. The report explores how innovation was deployed and identifies opportunities for further innovation in retrofit.

Opportunities for further analysis

The Retrofit for the Future data set is available on the embed database for further analysis. Researchers may be interested to:

  • Find out more about environmental conditions, which are considered at a high level in this analysis
  • Analyse the extent to which power generated on site was exported to the Grid
  • Compare the outcomes in properties with different packages of measures or where only limited measures were implemented
  • Explore the costs of retrofit. Further analysis is already under way.

Low Carbon Domestic Retrofit Guides : The Institute for Sustainability produced a set of guides based on the experiences of the Retrofit for the Future programme. They detail the business opportunities that retrofit offers, and provide practical and commercially focused advice and best practice to both trades and professions including architects, surveyors, builders, project managers, plumbers and electricians.
